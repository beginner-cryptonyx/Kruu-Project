The Vaccine Range
Influenza
Influenza, otherwise known as seasonal flu, is a contagious disease that infects the nose, throat, upper-respiratory tract and at times, the lungs. It generally causes mild illness but in some severe cases, it can cause hospitalisation and death.
Tdap
This vaccine combats against 3 diseases caused by bacteria; Tetanus, Diphtheria and Acellular Pertussis, hence the name (Tdap). These diseases can cause difficulty breathing, heart failure, paralysis and death. It's crucial to take this vaccine, not only to avoid these dangers, but also as Diphtheria and Pertussis are highly transmissible.
Hepatitis B
The Hepatitis B virus is a virus that attacks the liver and can cause damage to the liver that can even lead to liver failure. It is transmitted through blood or other bodily fluids, from mother to baby, or using injections, tattooing or piercing. This deadly disease can survive outside the body for atleast 7 days and has an incubation period of minimum 30 days.
HPV
HPV or the Human Papillomavirus isa disease transmitted sexually from an infected person that can cause cervical, anal and other cancers if present in the body for long periods. Taking the vaccine can help prevent this.
Varicella
The Varicella vaccine helps combat chicken pox, a highly contagious disease that can cause rashes all over the body and itching. It must be taken by children, adolescents and adults who have never gotten the vaccine or the disease previously.
Typhoid
The typhoid vaccine can help prevent typhoid vaccine caused by a bacteria multiplying in the body of an infected individual. Transmitted through contaminated food and water, this disease can go on for weeks or months without treatment and can eventually result in death. Individuals travelling to endemic areas are highly susceptible and must take this vaccine.
Covid-19
COVID-19 is a disease, its full name being "coronavirus disease," caused by the virus SARS-CoV-2. The virus easily spreads through small liquid droplets when an infected individual coughs,sneezes, sneezes or breathes. Isolation when you feel sick, wearing a mask, and social distancing are as crucial as vaccination to help decrease its transmission.
Zoster
This vaccine helps protect against shingles, a viral infection that is caused by the same virus that causes chickenpox which reactivates after several years of staying inside your body. It is transmitted via contact with fluids to anyone who isn't immune to chickenpox. The virus load in the zoster vaccine is 10 to 14 fold higher as compared to the Varicella vaccine, and is also more efficient at preventing PHN (postherpetic neuralgia).
PCV
PCV or Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine helps combat pneumococcal disease and pneumonia. It has two main vaccines, PCV15 or PCV20. It is vita for those who have diabetes, heart disease, HIV, liver disease, lung disease, renal disease or a weakened immune system
MMR
This vaccine is needed to combat Mumps, Measles and Rubella. These diseases are spread through sneezing and coughing through a virus in the infected person’s saliva. This vaccine is often taken in 2 doses during young ages, but if not, it should be taken as an adult at the earliest, especially to combat chronic diseases such as asplenia, liver disease and renal disease.
Hepatitis A
The Hepatitis A vaccine can help combat a serious liver disease, Hepatitis A. It is often transmitted through close contact with an infected person, or through small amounts of faeces in food, water or on objects. Most adults recover after several weeks of feeling sick with no real lasting damage to the liver. But in some cases, especially for adults over 50 with liver disease, hepatitis A can result in liver failure.
Yellow Fever
The yellow fever virus is found in tropical and subtropical areas of Africa and South America. The virus is spread to people by the bite of an infected mosquito. Illness ranges from a fever with aches and pains to severe liver disease with bleeding and yellowing skin (jaundice). There is no medicine to treat or cure infection, hence to prevent getting sick from yellow fever, use insect repellent, wear long-sleeved shirts and long pants, and get vaccinated.
Cholera
Cholera is an acute diarrhoeal infection caused by ingestion of food or water contaminated with the bacterium Vibrio cholerae. With an incubation period ranging from 12 hours to 5 days, infected individuals often do not show symptoms but are transmitted the disease through their faeces for 1 to 10 days. If not treated, the issue can escalate rapidly and lead to death.
