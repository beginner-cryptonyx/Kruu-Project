The Vaccine Range
Influenza
Influenza, otherwise known as the seasonal flu, is a contagious disease that infects the nose, throat and lungs. It can cause mild illness and in some severe cases, hospitalization and death. Adults need to take this vaccine once every year, and it is especially important for people with diabetes, heart disease, HIV, liver disease, lung disease, renal disease and a weakened immune system.

Hepatitis B
This virus can be transmitted through blood or other bodily fluids, from mother to baby, or using injections for several patients, and can often result in liver damage, cirrhosis, liver cancer and death. This deadly disease can survive outside the body for more than 7 days and has an incubation period of a minimum of 30 days. The vaccine can be given for those of the age 19 to 59 years lid, especially for people who have diabetes, HIV, liver disease or renal disease.
HPV
HPV or the Human Papillomavirus can cause cervical, anal and other cancers but also genital warts. It is transmitted sexually from an infected person. People who has HIV, liver disease, renal disease or a weakened immune system. Though it is often taken at the age of 11 to 12, it can also be taken through 26, but not beyond 26 without a doctor's recommendation.
Varicella
The Varicella vaccine helps combat chicken pox, a highly contagious disease that can cause rashes all over the body. It must be taken by children, adliescents and adults who have never gotten the vaccine or the disease previously. It is especially important for people who have renal disease and liver disease to take this dose.
Men-C-C
The Men-C-C vaccine, otherwise known as the Meningococcal Conjugate Vaccine should be taken to protect against meningitis and other meningococcal disease. Commonly a viral infection, it is caused by the inflammation of the fluid and membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. Though the first dose is generally for kids of age 11 and 12, and the second dose at 16, it is important for people who have HIV or asplenia to take this vaccine.
MMR
This vaccine is needed to combat Mumps, Measles and Rubella. These diseases are spread through sneezing and coughing through a virus in the infected person’s saliva. This vaccine is often taken in 2 doses during young ages, but if not, it should be taken as an adult at the earliest, especially to combat chronic diseases such as asplenia, liver disease and renal disease. 
Zoster
This vaccine helps protect against shingles, a viral infection caused by the same virus that causes chickenpox and stays in your body and reactivates after several years. It is transmitted via contact with fluids to anyone who isn't immune to chickenpox. This vaccine should be taken for healthy adults of age 50 years and lider, especially for those who have diabetes, heart disease, liver disease, lung disease and renal disease.
